## When you need a commercial licence

You need a commercial agreement before you can:

- Offer TestSuits (or a derivative) to third parties as a hosted or managed
  service — for example, running a multi-tenant SaaS where external customers
  sign up and use TestSuits.
- Embed TestSuits inside a product you sell that is "primarily" a QA platform.
- Redistribute a modified version under a different licence.
- Remove the branding or the LICENSE / NOTICE files and ship the result
  commercially.

If any of those describe what you want to do, please get in touch **before**
deploying — it is always easier to sort out licensing in advance.

## How to request a licence

Email the Licensor with a short description of your intended use:

- **Name of your organisation** and, if relevant, a URL
- **Use case** — what you want to do that isn't covered by the Additional Use
  Grant
- **Expected scale** — number of tenants / users / deployments
- **Hosting** — self-hosted, your cloud, your customers' cloud, shared SaaS

Contributions are welcome, but to keep the project legally coherent all pull
requests must be accompanied by a Developer Certificate of Origin sign-off
(see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)). By submitting a PR you confirm that
you wrote the change or have the right to contribute it, and that it may be
redistributed under the project's current licence **and** under the Change
License named in the LICENSE file.
